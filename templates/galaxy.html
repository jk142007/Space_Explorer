What is a Galaxy?

A galaxy is a huge collection of gas, dust, stars, and dark matter held together by gravity. Galaxies can contain billions or even trillions of stars. Our own Milky Way is a galaxy, and it's only one of billions in the universe!

Types of Galaxies

  • Spiral Galaxies: Like the Milky Way, they have beautiful swirling arms.
  • Elliptical Galaxies: Shaped like stretched spheres with older stars.
  • Irregular Galaxies: No definite shape, often distorted by collisions.

Biggest Known Galaxies

The largest known galaxy is IC 1101, which is 6 million light-years wide! Some galaxies collide and merge, creating even larger galaxies over billions of years.

How Far Away?

Many galaxies are millions or even billions of light-years away from Earth. Looking at them is like looking back in time, since the light took so long to reach us.
