What is a Black Hole?

A black hole is a region in space where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape from it. They are formed when massive stars collapse at the end of their life cycles.

Event Horizon

The event horizon is the boundary around a black hole. Once anything crosses this invisible line, it can never escape the black hole's pull.

Singularity

At the center of a black hole lies the singularity ‚Äî a point of infinite density where the laws of physics as we know them break down.

Types of Black Holes

  • Stellar Black Holes: Formed from collapsed stars.
  • Supermassive Black Holes: Found at the centers of galaxies, millions or billions of times the mass of the Sun.
  • Intermediate Black Holes: A middle category still being studied.

Can We See Black Holes?

We can't see black holes directly, but we can observe the effects they have on nearby stars and matter. The famous first image of a black hole was captured by the Event Horizon Telescope in 2019.
